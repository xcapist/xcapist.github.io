Lingus

I picked this song for the incredible fusion of mutliple elements and styles. The style is distictively jazz, but the delivery is funky, and alternate to rock elements frequently. The syhtnesized sounds and the keyboard solos at the end were just toppings to an outstanding piece.

Chonks

This song emphasizes their commitment to mixing all styles. This recording was done later than Lingus, and the change in style is very apparent from just jazz to more Jazz-rock. It also features a violin, an uncommon instrument today but a core element in this version.

Shofukan

The song begins more orchestral in focus, and is better shown through the violin section being much larger. In many of their songs Funk takes the lead, but this allowed a more ethereal rock and light orchestra to create more dissonance and counter melodies than most bands would explore. By midway through the song, the melody completely overrun by the synth and feels much chunkier, completely unrecognizable to the inital notes.

Tarova

I chose the Tiny Desk Concert version because the members chose to improvise before starting the song. This is important because Tiny Desk Concert has their own band, and their expressions are the best. Even around other talented successful musicians, their music style is a clear and unique thing to behold.

What About Me?

On first listen, this begins as a full rock song with a more modern usage of jazz elements. And if you're still listening by 2:20, the song yet again has shifted towards the swing end of jazz at which point snaps back to rock with an incredible drum solo. This virtuosity is what kept me interested in listening, waiting for the next "magic trick" they would add next.
