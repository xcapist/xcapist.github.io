About The "Kingmaker" artist

Joel Ross is a Chicago-born, Brooklyn-based vibraphonist whose sound is centerd in the post-bop tradition. He group up on the South Side of Chicago and is one of two twin sons to Chicago Police officers. He and his brother learned to play at the age of 3 and regularly played at their church, of which the father was the choir director for. He began to focus on the vibraphone after joining All City concert and jazz bands, which would lead to opportunities to play with groups at the Jazz Institute of Chicago and later enrolling at the Chicago Institute of the Arts (with the school's partnership with the Thelonious Monk Institute of Jazz). He would get pockets of mentorship from greats such as Herbie Hancock and Stefon Harris, who would later encourage him to audition for his band, the Brubeck Institute Jazz Quintet at the University of the Pacific which would furter develop his skill and ability to coordinate larger groups, ultimately cuminating into the formation of his "Good Vibes" ensemble. Thus far, he has an album on the Billboard Top Ten (Kingmaker) which debuted his music as a band leader.
