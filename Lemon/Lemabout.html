About The "Father of the Texas Blues"

Lemon Henry "Blind Lemon" Jefferson was a blues and gospel singer-songwriter and guitarist whose distinctive high-pitched voice and intricate style of guitar playing cemented him as one of the most prominent artists in the early blues movement and early commercial recording. Even at an early age, Jefferson would busk on the streets in Dallas and other East Texas towns and eventually settled into the Deep Ellum section of Dallas arund 1917 as part of the growing blues movement. He would eventually bring himself and his Texas blues to Chicago around December 1925 - January 1926, where he recorded his first tracks, and would remain there until his death in 1929.

  • Achievements

    • One of few sucessful Solo Guitarist and Male Vocalist in the early Commercial Recording Industry
    • Recored about 100 songs/43 albums between 1926-1929

  • Awards

    • His 1927 recording of "Matchbox Blues" is a part of the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's 500 Songs that Shaped Rock and Roll
    • Inducted into the Blues Hall of Fame in 1980

  • Interesting Facts

    • One of eight children to southern sharecroppers
    • Blind since birth much like Stevie Wonder
    • Early prominent figure of the blues in Deep Ellum, Dallas
    • Recorded both secular and spiritual music, starting his publishing career with "I Want to Be Like Jesus in My Heart" and "All I Want Is That Pure Religion"
    • Published under the pseudonym Deacon L. J. Bates early in his career before using his real name
